# How to Take Smart Notes

Notes on the Zettelkasten method — a system for building a personal knowledge base that thinks back at you. #productivity #learning #writing

---

## What is Zettelkasten?

Zettelkasten (German for "slip box") is a note-taking system developed by sociologist Niklas Luhmann, who used it to write over 70 books and 400 academic papers. The core idea: notes aren't storage — they're thinking tools. A well-linked slip box surfaces unexpected connections and generates ideas you wouldn't have had otherwise.

---

## Types of Notes

**Fleeting Notes**
Quick captures — anything that crosses your mind. Inbox only. Process within 24–48 hours or discard. Don't let these pile up.

**Literature Notes**
Written while reading. One source per note. Paraphrase in your own words — don't copy. Ask: what does this mean for my thinking?

**Permanent Notes (Zettels)**
The atomic unit. One idea per note, written as if for a stranger. Self-contained, linked, densely connected. These are the real output.

**Project Notes**
Temporary. Tied to a specific deliverable. Archive or delete when the project ends.

---

## Principles

1. **Write in your own words** — if you can't paraphrase it, you don't understand it yet
2. **One idea per note** — atomic notes are easier to link and reuse
3. **Link liberally** — value comes from connections, not from notes in isolation
4. **Write for your future self** — context you omit today will be opaque in six months
5. **Trust the system** — capture first, organize later, never upfront

---

## My Workflow

1. Read with a pen (or highlights) — capture fleeting notes as I go
2. After each session, convert highlights into literature notes in my own words
3. Ask: what permanent notes does this connect to or create?
4. Write permanent notes and link them to existing notes
5. Let the structure emerge — no forced categories
