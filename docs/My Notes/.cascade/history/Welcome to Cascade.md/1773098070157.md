# Welcome to Cascade

Welcome to your second brain. This vault is where ideas live, connect, and grow into something useful.

Cascade is built around one principle: **notes should link to each other**. A note in isolation is just a file. A note connected to ten other notes is part of a thinking system. Use `[[wiki-links]]` liberally — the goal is to build a web of knowledge, not a filing cabinet.

Start from the [[Dashboard]] to get an overview of what's active. If you're new to this style of note-taking, [[How to Take Smart Notes]] is the best place to begin.

---

## How This Vault Is Organized

```
My Notes/
├── Inbox/        ← Raw captures, unprocessed ideas
├── Projects/     ← Active work with a finish line
├── Areas/        ← Ongoing responsibilities (health, career, finances)
├── Resources/    ← Reference material, book notes, evergreen notes
├── Daily Notes/  ← Daily log and reflection
└── Templates/    ← Note templates
```

This follows a loose **PARA** structure (Projects, Areas, Resources, Archives). Don't stress about perfect organization — linking matters more than folders.

---

## Getting Started

1. Open [[Dashboard]] and skim what's active.
2. Whenever a thought strikes, drop it in `Inbox/` first and process it later.
3. Finished a book? Capture key ideas as a dedicated note and link it from [[Dashboard]].
4. When two ideas feel related, link them — even if you're not sure why yet.

---

## Inspiration Behind This Vault

Two books shaped how this vault works:

- **[[Atomic Habits]]** — Small, consistent inputs compound. Even 10 minutes of daily note review builds a powerful knowledge base over time.
- **[[Deep Work]]** — Focused capture beats scattered multitasking. Write notes when you're actually thinking, not as a procrastination ritual.

> "Notes aren't just storage. They're a thinking partner."

The goal isn't to record everything — it's to record the things that *surprise* you, *challenge* you, or *connect* unexpectedly to something else.

---

## Tips

- Use `#tags` for broad categories, but rely on `[[links]]` for real connections.
- Don't rename notes carelessly — links will break.
- Revisit old notes before creating new ones. You'll often find you've thought about something before.
- The [[Dashboard]] is your daily home base. Keep it honest and up to date.
