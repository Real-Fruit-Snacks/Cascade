## The Distracted Mind — Aeon, Feb 2026

A piece on how smartphones have restructured attention at a neurological level, not just a behavioral one. Ties directly into what Cal Newport argues in [[Deep Work]].

> "We are not failing to focus because we lack willpower. We are failing to focus because we have engineered environments that make focus structurally impossible."

**My take:** The environment design angle is underrated. Newport talks about this too — remove the temptation, don't rely on discipline. Need to revisit my desk setup and phone habits. Link to [[Health & Fitness]] eventually (sleep and attention are connected).

## Tiny Experiments — Ness Labs, Jan 2026

About running small personal experiments rather than committing to huge habit overhauls. Very aligned with [[Atomic Habits]] — the idea that you don't need to be sure something will work, you just need to make it cheap to try.

> "A 30-day experiment removes the identity pressure of 'I'm becoming a runner' and replaces it with 'I'm testing whether running helps me think.'"

**Worth turning into a note.** The framing of *experiments vs. commitments* is something I keep coming back to. Less shame when you stop, more data either way.

## The Burnout Paradox — Harvard Business Review, Dec 2025

Argues that high performers burn out not from *too much work* but from work that feels **meaningless or uncontrollable**. Autonomy is the key variable. Connects to [[Career Development]] — specifically thinking about which parts of my job feel chosen vs. imposed.

> "Exhaustion is not the enemy of performance. Purposelessness is."

**Action:** Write a note in [[Career Development]] about the distinction between exhaustion and disengagement. These feel similar from the inside but have completely different solutions.
